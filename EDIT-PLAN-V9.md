## Global Find-and-Fix Patterns

These appear throughout and should be addressed in every chapter:

| Pattern | Action | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| "Not X. Not Y. Z." construction | Reduce by 70% | "Not a hug. Not the polite kind... I HELD her." — Keep 30% of these. Cut the rest. |
| Sentences that begin "That's..." after dialogue/moment | Cut 50% | "That's my marriage in a screenshot." — Some work. Most over-explain. |
| Aphorisms/tweetable lines | Cut 60% | "Theories are just loneliness wearing a lab coat." — Save these for 2-3 per chapter max. |
| "I know how it sounds" / "I need you to know" / "Let me explain" | Cut 80% | Direct address to reader breaks the spell when overused. Once or twice in the book, fine. |
| Metaphors at moments of peak emotion | Replace with physical sensation | When Ari is in pain, describe his body (jaw, hands, chest), not a literary image. |
| Explaining after showing | Cut the explanation | If a scene or dialogue exchange makes the point, don't follow it with a paragraph restating the point. |
