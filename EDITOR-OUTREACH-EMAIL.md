## Version A: Cold outreach (Reedsy, LinkedIn, direct email)

**Subject:** Developmental edit inquiry — 64K literary fiction novel

Hi [Name],

I'm looking for a developmental editor for my debut novel, *The Woman in My Phone* (64,000 words, literary fiction).

**The book:** A South Asian diaspora male protagonist in Seattle conducts a five-year affair with a woman in India — told entirely through the artifacts of a digital life (TikTok analytics, WhatsApp messages, voice notes, search histories). First-person past tense, with two close-third POV chapters from the women's perspectives. Six parts, 31 chapters.

**Themes:** Obsession, control, digital intimacy, diaspora identity, masculinity, reckoning.

**Comp:** *Normal People* for the age of infinite scroll.

**What I'm looking for:** A full developmental edit — structure, arcs, pacing, emotional stakes. Not copy editing (that's done). I want to know where a reader skims, which scenes feel unearned, and whether the unconventional format serves the story.

**What I'll send:** Full manuscript (.docx), a submission brief with 8 specific concerns, and a synopsis.

**Deliverables I'd like:** 10–15 page editorial letter + inline comments. Open to a follow-up call.

**Budget:** $800–$1,500 USD (negotiable based on scope).

**Timeline:** Flexible — 4–6 weeks turnaround is fine.

Would you be available? If so, I'd love to send a sample (first 3 chapters) so you can confirm it's a good fit before we commit.

Thank you,
Abhi

## Version B: Upwork job post

**Title:** Developmental Edit — 64K Literary Fiction Novel (South Asian diaspora)

**Description:**

I need a developmental editor for my debut literary fiction novel (64,000 words, 31 chapters).

The book is a love story told through TikTok analytics, WhatsApp messages, voice notes, and search histories — a South Asian diaspora male protagonist in Seattle conducting a five-year affair with a woman in India. Themes: obsession, control, digital intimacy, masculinity, reckoning. Comp: *Normal People* for the age of infinite scroll.

I'm NOT looking for copy editing or proofreading. I need structural/developmental feedback:
- Where does the pacing drag?
- Do all three character arcs land?
- Which scenes feel unearned?
- Does the mixed-media format serve the story?

**Deliverables:**
- 10–15 page editorial letter (high-level assessment + prioritized recommendations)
- Inline comments in the manuscript (scene-level, not line-level)

**Requirements:**
- Experience editing literary fiction (not genre/romance/corporate)
- Publishing house experience preferred (Penguin, HarperCollins, Juggernaut, etc.)
- Familiarity with South Asian/diaspora narratives is a plus
- Please include in your proposal: 2–3 literary fiction titles you've edited and your typical turnaround time

**Budget:** $800–$1,500 USD (fixed price)
**Timeline:** 4–6 weeks
