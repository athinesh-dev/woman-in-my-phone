## Specific concerns (what I want you to focus on)

1. **Part 4 pacing (Ch 14–16):** Does the "soon / not yet / pattern" compression across 18 months feel like rhythm or repetition? Ch 14 was trimmed but may still sag.

2. **Priya subplot landing:** Priya's POV chapter (Ch 22) is late in the book and she appears mostly offstage before it. Does she arrive with enough weight, or does her chapter feel unearned?

3. **Pre-jail interiority (Ch 17–19):** Ari's spiral from paternity revelation to voice note — is the interior degradation textured enough, or does it move too fast from "discovered the lie" to "sent the voice note"?

4. **Post-divorce daughter scene (Ch 26):** Recently added. Does it earn its placement? Does Anika's dialogue feel age-appropriate for eleven?

5. **Recovery pacing (Part 6):** Eight chapters of denouement. Does it earn its length or does the reader want the book to end sooner?

6. **Meera's late-stage interiority (Ch 24–25):** Her decision to leave Vikram is shown through artifacts but not narrated from inside her. Is that restraint effective, or a gap?

7. **Diaspora theme integration:** Does the Tamil/Hindi cultural specificity feel organic throughout, or bolted on? The Ch 26 sambar passage is the thematic anchor — does the thread leading to it feel consistent?

8. **Opening strength:** Ch 1 opens cold on TikTok analytics. Does it hook? Is the tonal contract clear by page 3?
