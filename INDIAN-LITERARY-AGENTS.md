# Indian Literary Agents

Guide for Indian-based literary agents who can represent fiction in the Indian market and coordinate international rights.

**The honest truth:** The Indian literary agent market is tiny — maybe 4-5 credible agencies total, and only 2 (Writer's Side, Jacaranda) have strong literary fiction credentials. Many Indian literary fiction authors of the previous generation went straight to London agents because the infrastructure didn't exist in India.

---

## India-Based Agencies

### 1. Writer's Side (Kanishka Gupta) — Delhi

- **Founded:** 2010
- **Claims:** Largest literary agency in South Asia
- **Notable clients:** Avni Doshi (Booker-shortlisted, *Burnt Sugar*), Shehan Karunatilaka (Booker winner, *Seven Moons of Maali Almeida*), Anees Salim (Sahitya Akademi), Jerry Pinto, Ranjit Hoskote
- **Handles:** Indian subcontinent rights primarily; coordinates international rights via co-agents
- **Submit:** Synopsis + author bio + first 3 chapters (Word) to kanishka500@gmail.com
- **Commission:** 15% domestic, 20% foreign (standard)
- **Caveat:** Kanishka Gupta was subject of #MeToo allegations in 2018. Multiple women accused him of harassment. He continues to operate.

### 2. Siyahi (Mita Kapur) — Jaipur

- **Founded:** 2007
- **Notable clients:** Devdutt Pattanaik, Tabish Khair, KP Ramanunni
- **Handles:** Indian AND international rights. Sub-agents in US, UK, Spain, France, Italy, Korea, Germany, Brazil. Also handles film/TV adaptation rights.
- **Submit:** Fiction submissions accepted only 10th–15th of each month. First 3 chapters + synopsis + author bio. Single Word doc, font 14, double-spaced. Email: mitakapur@siyahi.in. Response: 12–14 weeks.
- **Caveat:** Roster leans heavier toward nonfiction, mythology, celebrity books. Literary fiction is not their primary strength.

### 3. Jacaranda Agency (Jayapriya Vasudevan) — Chennai

- **Founded:** ~2012
- **Notable clients:** Perumal Murugan (*One Part Woman*), S. Hareesh (JCB Prize winner, *Moustache*)
- **Handles:** Indian subcontinent rights, translation rights (between Indian languages), coordinates with international agents for world English
- **Strength:** True literary fiction sensibility. Translation expertise.
- **Caveat:** Small operation, hard to reach. Website may be down. Try via Jaipur Literature Festival network or publishing industry contacts.

### 4. Labyrinth Literary Agency (Anish Chandy) — Delhi

- **Founded:** 2014
- **Background:** Former Senior Commissioning Editor at Penguin Random House India, then Juggernaut
- **Notable clients:** Nikita Singh, Sanjay Manjrekar, Azim Premji biography. 100+ published titles.
- **Handles:** Print (domestic + international), audiobook (Audible, Storytel, Kuku FM), web series/film adaptation, translation
- **Submit:** Synopsis (1–2 pages) + chapters 1–2 + author bio + word count. Word doc, Garamond 12. Email: submissions@labyrinthagency.com. Response: ~2 weeks.
- **Caveat:** Skews commercial fiction, business books, celebrity memoirs. Less proven with literary fiction.

## UK/US Agents Who Handle Major Indian Literary Fiction

### David Godwin Associates (London)

- **Clients:** Arundhati Roy, Kiran Desai, Amitav Ghosh
- **Handles:** World English rights. Sells Indian subcontinent rights to Penguin India separately.
- **Submission:** Effectively closed to unsolicited. Referral or prize-longlist discovery only.
- **Reality:** The gold standard. Near-impossible for debuts without a referral.

### The Wylie Agency (New York / London)

- **Clients:** Salman Rushdie, Jhumpa Lahiri, Mohsin Hamid
- **Submission:** Highly selective, referral-based.
- **Reality:** Unlikely for debuts. They poach established authors. Relevant if your book breaks out in India first.

---

## Do You Need an Indian Agent?

**Probably not.** Indian publishers accept direct submissions (no agent required). An agent is worth it only if you want:
- Someone negotiating your contract terms
- International rights coordination from India
- Film/TV adaptation deal-making

A publishing lawyer (₹10,000–25,000 / $120–$300) can review the contract for cheaper than an agent's 15% commission.

---

## Commission Structure (Industry Standard)

- **15%** on domestic (Indian subcontinent) deals
- **20%** on international/translation deals (split with sub-agent)
- **No reading fees** — any agency charging upfront is not legitimate
