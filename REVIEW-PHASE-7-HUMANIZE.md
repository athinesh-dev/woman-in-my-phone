## 2. Cuts That Left Holes

**Old:** Included "I didn't shower for four days the first week. Then I did, and afterward I felt better the way you feel better after taking a shower, which is not the same as feeling good."
**New:** "I didn't shower for four days the first week. Then I did."

The cut is correct — the "which is not the same as feeling good" was commentary. But "Then I did" as a standalone sentence followed by a paragraph break reads slightly abrupt. Not broken — just a beat shorter than the surrounding prose rhythm. The paragraph that follows (granola bars, eggs, dal) picks up fine, so this is cosmetic, not structural. **No action needed.**

### Everything else flows cleanly.

No paragraphs trail off. No mid-thought stops. Every cut location reads as a complete beat in context.

## 3. Surviving Patterns That Should Also Be Cut

I looked specifically at the depth-add scenes (not the original author's prose). Three items:

### Pattern A: "Not X. Y." constructions

These are subtler than the X/Y aphorisms that got cut, but a few remain:

- Ch 22, line ~4892: "That was the worst moment of those two weeks. Worse than the bathroom floor message. Because the bathroom floor was a thing that had happened to Meera. The cereal bowl was a thing my daughter was doing to survive me."

The first two sentences are fine — they rank the moment. The last two sentences are a constructed parallel ("a thing that happened to Meera" / "a thing my daughter was doing to survive me") that has the AI editor's fingerprint. The cereal bowl scene itself (daughter pouring cereal, the *okay* voice, taking the bowl to her room) already landed. **Consider cutting the last two sentences.** "That was the worst moment of those two weeks" is enough.

### Pattern B: One remaining "caption" line

- Ch 22, line ~4894: "I should have known then. I didn't."

This is a retrospective narrator's caption — the kind of line the AI editor used in other places that got cut. It's borderline. The "I should have known" construction appears in the original author's prose too, so it's less clearly an AI tell. **Flag but don't cut unless you want to be aggressive.**

### Pattern C: No other aphorisms found

The rest of the depth-add prose reads clean. The AI editor's self-audit was thorough. I specifically checked the Ch 19 "two life rafts lashed together" passage (line ~4455-4459) — that's the original author's voice, not the AI editor's, so it's correctly untouched.
