## 1. Does the new depth-add prose match the benchmark voice?

### Ch 23 (Priya) — YES. Strongest new material in the book.

The October 2016 doorway scene lands because of its mundane specificity: "Did you renew the car tabs?" is exactly the register this book operates in. The burnt mustard seeds and starting the tempering over — that's the novel's signature move, where physical domestic action carries emotional weight without the prose pointing at it.

The 3 AM bathroom hair-holding scene works as the emotional hinge of the chapter. Priya keeping that memory "the way some people keep a photograph" gives her interiority without breaking the restraint of her voice. And the payoff — "By 2023 she stopped taking the photograph out at all. It hurt more than it helped" — is devastating in the understated way Ch 13 is devastating.

The cold withdrawal section (body, then money, then domestic architecture) is where Priya becomes a full character. "She was an engineer. She understood load-bearing systems." That line earns the analytical tone that follows. The cold satisfaction passage — "she felt a specific, cold satisfaction. The satisfaction of finally being the one who withholds" — is honest in a way the book needs. It refuses to make Priya a saint.

**One note:** Ch 23 runs slightly more psychological/analytical than Ch 13 (Meera's Year), which operates almost entirely through sensation and action. But this reads as intentional character differentiation, not a voice break. Priya thinks in systems. Meera thinks in the body. The prose reflects who's narrating.

### Ch 11 opening (TikTok Live scene) — Voice-consistent.

The unsent WhatsApp message — deleted character by character — is a strong physical action that mirrors the novel's recurring move of showing digital artifacts being created, read, and destroyed. "Nobody heard" as a standalone paragraph has the right weight.

## 4. Does "But last Tuesday, I didn't" land?

**YES.** Here's why it works:

The sequence before it does the necessary undermining. "I love her like a King now. Most days." — the qualifier keeps the ending honest. Then the last truth: 45 minutes staring at a photo, 8 seconds on the green button. The reader watches Ari almost relapse in real time. The thumb on the button. The counting.

Then the definition: "That's abundance. Not the elimination of hunger. The choice, every single time, to set the phone down anyway."

Then the admission: "Some days I pick it back up by midnight."

Then the landing: **"But last Tuesday, I didn't."**

No grand declaration. No lyrical sweep. Just a small, specific, recent act of restraint from a man who's been unreliable about restraint for 31 chapters. The tense is right (past simple, not present — it's a report, not a vow). The word "Tuesday" is right — ordinary, not symbolic. And the trailing Author's Note operates in a different register (the author, not Ari), so it doesn't dilute the close. The moved dedication — "For everyone who's been the villain of someone else's story and didn't know it yet" — echoes the novel's thesis without restating it.

**Nothing after that line should change.**

## One Flag: Mountain Metaphor Echo (Ch 29 → Ch 32)

Ch 29 (Forty) and Ch 32 (Abundance) both contain the biryani-alone / mountains-from-window / fullness motif. That's intentional repetition — the birthday dinner in Ch 29 is the rehearsal, the closing in Ch 32 is the test.

But the mountain metaphor appears in both:
- Ch 29: "the mountains were still there in the dark. I couldn't see them. I didn't need to."
- Ch 32: "the mountain is always there whether you can see it or not"

If a careful reader notices, it could feel like the prose is repeating a metaphor rather than developing it.

**Recommendation:** One read-through to see if the Ch 32 mountain line can carry a slightly different payload than the Ch 29 version, so the echo feels like growth instead of recurrence. The Ch 29 version is about not needing to see. The Ch 32 version could carry something new — perhaps about the rain, or the choice to stand at the window anyway, or the difference between faith and certainty. Small adjustment, not a rewrite.
